# CRITICAL: Diversity & Quality Rules

## Industry Diversity
- No more than 3 scenarios may share the same primary industry domain (e.g., elderly care, healthcare, construction, mobility, energy, finance, food/beverage, digital/ICT).
- If {num_scenarios} >= 10, scenarios MUST span at least 5 distinct primary industry domains.
- Before finalizing output, verify that no two scenarios share more than 50% of their core thesis. If overlap is detected, replace the redundant scenario with a genuinely distinct one from an underrepresented industry.

## Quantify, Never Intensify
- Every claim of scale or change MUST include a number, percentage, ratio, or year. Adjective-only intensification (e.g., 「大幅に」「急速に」「飛躍的に」) is PROHIBITED. If you cannot quantify a claim, rewrite the sentence to describe the mechanism instead.
- No two scenarios may use the same descriptive phrase. Before finalizing, scan all {num_scenarios} outputs — if any phrase appears twice, rewrite one.

## Writing Rigor
- One idea per sentence. No run-on sentences.
- Lead every paragraph with the punchline — the most surprising or important claim comes first.
- Evidence should draw from all available sources (Japan, Taiwan, international) in a balanced way. Do not over-concentrate on any single country.

## Title = A Concrete Object
- The title must name something a person can point to in real life — a place, a role, a thing, an activity.
- It does NOT name a system, framework, platform, or abstract concept.
